Fried Rice

Fried Rice is a versatile, globally beloved dish, popularized in Asian cuisine. Originally from China, it quickly became a worldwide staple due to its ease, adaptability, and ability to transform leftovers into a delicious meal. Classic versions include vegetables, eggs, and optionally, meat or seafood, stir-fried with cooked rice and soy sauce for umami depth.

Ingredients:

  • 3 cups cooked, cooled long-grain rice
  • 2 tbsp oil (vegetable or sesame)
  • 2 eggs, beaten
  • 1 cup mixed vegetables (carrot, peas, bell pepper, corn)
  • 1/2 cup chopped scallions
  • 1 small onion, diced
  • 2-3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tsp oyster sauce (optional)
  • 1/2 tsp white pepper
  • Salt, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Heat 1 tbsp oil in a wok. Pour in eggs; scramble until just set, then remove.
  2. Add more oil. Saut√© garlic and onion until fragrant.
  3. Add vegetables and stir-fry 3-4 minutes.
  4. Add rice; break up clumps and mix with vegetables.
  5. Return eggs to the pan, add soy sauce, optional oyster sauce, white pepper, and salt.
  6. Stir-fry on high heat for 2-3 minutes. Add scallions; toss once more.
  7. Serve hot as a main or side dish.

Chicken Biriyani

Chicken Biryani is an iconic South Asian one-pot rice and meat dish, celebrated for its fragrant layers and blend of aromatic spices. Its origins are traced to Persian influences brought to India by the Mughals, combining Persian pilaf with Indian flavors. Over centuries, numerous local adaptations have emerged across the subcontinent, but all emphasize spiced chicken, basmati rice, saffron, and herbsâ€”often cooked together using the revered dum (steam) method.

Ingredients:

  • 1 kg bone-in chicken pieces
  • 2 cups basmati rice
  • 1 cup yogurt
  • 2 large onions, thinly sliced
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 2 tbsp ginger garlic paste
  • 1/2 cup vegetable oil or ghee
  • 1/2 cup chopped mint leaves
  • 1/2 cup chopped coriander leaves
  • 1-2 green chilies, slit
  • 1 tsp saffron strands, soaked in 1/4 cup milk
  • Whole spices: 4 green cardamoms, 6 cloves, 2 bay leaves, 1-inch cinnamon stick, 1 star anise
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 2 tsp red chili powder
  • 2 tsp garam masala
  • 2 tsp coriander powder
  • Salt, to taste
  • Juice of 1 lemon

Instructions:

  1. Wash and soak rice for 30 minutes.
  2. Marinate chicken with yogurt, half the ginger garlic paste, half the chili powder, turmeric, garam masala, salt, and lemon juice. Rest for at least 1 hour.
  3. Fry sliced onions in oil/ghee until golden brown; set aside half for layering.
  4. In the same pan, add whole spices, remaining ginger garlic paste, green chilies, and tomatoes. Cook till tomatoes are soft.
  5. Add marinated chicken and cook until chicken is mostly done and oil separates. Add mint and coriander leaves.
  6. Boil rice with salt and whole spices until 70% cooked. Drain.
  7. Layer chicken and gravy, fried onions, and partially cooked rice in a large pot. Top with saffron milk, remaining fried onions, and some fresh herbs.
  8. Cover tightly (seal lid with dough if possible), reduce heat, and cook on dum (low heat) for 20-30 minutes until rice and chicken are tender.
  9. Rest 10 minutes before serving. Serve with raita or salad.
